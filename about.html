Life Story

Ashin Zargara was born in April 1955, to a farming family, in a small town located in central Myanmar (Burma), as a Buddhist novice at the age of ten. He ordained as a Buddhist Monk at the age of twenty. Since then, he practices a strict religious and ascetic lifestyle. (A monk may be a person who decides to dedicate his life to serving all other living beings, or to be an ascetic who voluntarily chooses to leave mainstream society and live his life in prayer and contemplation. The concept is ancient and can be seen in many religions and in philosophy.)

He moved in Yangon, a capital city of Myanmar (Burma), in 1983. He started writing poetry and novels in the 1985s. He published mindfulness books, poems and wrote peace activists in magazines. He is actively involved in Burmese politics activities. He protested against the government in the 8888 Uprising that peaked in August 1988. The uprising ended in September after a bloody military coup. Thousands of deaths have been attributed to the military during this uprising. In 1999, he worked as an editorial for many newspapers. In 2007, led peaceful demonstrations in Myanmar that were crushed by the countryâ€™s military regime. The various protests were led by students, political activists, including Buddhist monks, women and took the form of a campaign of civil resistance. He was at the forefront of the struggle for independence and democracy. In response to the protests, dozens of protesters were arrested or detained. He was hiding in a safe place for a while. Nonetheless, to keep against the military dictators he fled to the United States in 2010.

He is currently residing in Milwaukee, Wisconsin where he is continuing to practice Buddhism, author mind peacefulness books, and contribute on getting Myanmar democracy.
